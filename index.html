    Concept

    Traditional music presentation formats are tired. Standard audience/performer positioning is restrictive, hierarchical and separated. In a contemporary landscape where artists are continually pushing sonic boundaries, to utilise accepted templates of presentation is to limit potential audience experience. To push the speakers to the boundaries, to place the performer in the centre of the room sharing the same sound-field as the listener, is to offer an equalized and expanded and performance/audience experience.

    System Design

    The Stage will be located in the centre of the room with the audience in the round. Full 4:1 surround sound system + Mono centre cluster system will be available for sound output. Each surround system will be a high output power line array - not small satellites systems. Mono cluster system will be 4 x high output power line array systems- not small in-fill systems. Artists can choose to send audio to the surround sound system or the mono system, or both

    Artist Brief

    With the performer area placed in the middle of the room and the audience in the round; we are seeking artists who are interested in producing new compositions or translating existing performances into an extended and immersive presentation format. Artists are provided with the opportunity to compose and perform work through a very high fidelity surround sound system with enough headroom to allow for very high sound pressure levels. In order not to restrict artists to specifically composed 4:1 performances, and to allow for coherent playback of non-surround material within the same audience/stage set-up (pre-existing material composed for stereo systems…etc), there will be an alternative audio system located in-front of the stage edge. This means there is still opportunity for an original audience experience without the problem of beats going out of time between distantly placed surround sound speakers. These systems, when combined, can be used as a 5.1 system with the centre cluster becoming the centre channel, thus giving artists yet more flexibility in composition and performance.
